# Objective
The objective of this project is to build a machine learning model to answer two business questions using the Telco Customer Churn dataset:Which customers are likely to churn? (predictive) and Why are they likely to churn? (interpretability / driver analysis).
# Dataset
Source: IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset (downloaded from Kaggle, open access), including 7,043 customers, 21 features (after cleaning: 7,032 rows, 30 engineered features), features include: tenure, monthly charges, total charges, contract type, internet service, payment method, demographic data, etc.
# Tools & Technologies
Python3, Google Colab, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit‑learn (Logistic Regression, Random Forest), XGBoost, ROC‑AUC, classification metrics.
# Workflow
-Data loading & exploration: Load CSV, inspect missing values, data types, duplicates.Found 11 missing values in TotalCharges (empty strings).
-Data cleaning: Drop customerID (irrelevant). Convert TotalCharges to numeric, drop missing rows. Map Churn to 0/1. Create ordinal Contract_ordinal (0=Month‑to‑month, 1=One year, 2=Two year) and drop original Contract. One‑hot encode 14 categorical columns with drop_first=True. Final cleaned dataset: 7,032 rows, 30 features.
-Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Churn rate by contract type: 42.7% (month‑to‑month) vs 11.3% (one year) vs 2.8% (two year). Boxplots: churned customers have lower median tenure (~10 months) and higher median monthly charges (~$75). Categorical feature analysis: PaperlessBilling_Yes, Electronic check, Fiber optic associated with higher churn. Subgroup analysis (tenure<6 months & month‑to‑month contract): churn rate increases from 35% to 77% as monthly charges rise – key directional evidence.
-Model training & comparison: Train/test split (80/20, stratified). Scale numeric features (tenure, MonthlyCharges, TotalCharges). Train three models with class balancing. Evaluate using ROC‑AUC.
-Failed experiment – Logistic Regression coefficients: Extract coefficients (MonthlyCharges has negative coefficient (-0.502)). Contradicts EDA and subgroup analysis (higher charges, higher churn). Reason: linear model cannot handle interactions between MonthlyCharges, tenure, and Contract. Also multicollinearity with TotalCharges. Decision: Reject LR for interpretability despite higher AUC (0.835). Switch to Random Forest.
-Random Forest feature importance: Compute Gini impurity importance from balanced RF. Top features: TotalCharges (0.169), tenure (0.159), MonthlyCharges (0.155), Contract_ordinal (0.103). Direction confirmed by subgroup analysis (high monthly charges increase churn in new month‑to‑month customers).
-Identify highest‑risk customers: Predict churn probabilities on test set using RF. Sort descending, list top 10 customers. All have tenure=1 month, month‑to‑month contract, monthly charges 35–77, churn probability >0.97.
-Answer business questions: Which customers? – Provide table of top‑risk indices. Why? – Global drivers (feature importance + subgroup analysis) explain that low tenure, high monthly charges, and month‑to‑month contract dominate.
# Key Insights
Low tenure (especially 1 month) and month‑to‑month contract are the strongest churn signals. High monthly charges – contrary to the failed LR coefficient – increase churn risk when examined within the high‑risk subgroup (tenure<6, month‑to‑month). Fiber optic internet, electronic check payment, and lack of online security / tech support are secondary but important drivers. Logistic Regression can be misleading for interpretability when interactions and multicollinearity exist – even with good AUC. Random Forest + subgroup analysis provides a reliable, actionable explanation without needing complex SHAP (though SHAP could add per‑customer depth). Business action: Proactively target new month‑to‑month customers with high monthly charges → offer contract extensions, bundle security services, and encourage automatic payment methods.
